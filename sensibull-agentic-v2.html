Your daily workflow
Pre-marketBefore 9:15 AM — what to do
3 steps
1
Read the morning brief. Go to Morning Brief. The agent has already processed overnight data and identified today's setups. Read the 2 setup cards and the reasoning chain.
2
Verify in the option chain if you want. Go to Option Chain. The agent's reasoning references specific OI levels — you can verify them yourself here. Click any strike to add it to the builder.
3
Go to AI Recommendations. The agent has already structured the trade. Review the backtest (43 historical occurrences), check the reasoning, and either approve or click "Modify strikes" to change parameters before approving.
Market hours9:15 AM – 3:00 PM — what to do
4 steps · Most important
1
Approve the trade. On AI Recommendations, optionally modify strikes, then click "Approve & place". The executor places all legs simultaneously as a basket. All 4 fill within seconds.
2
Let the agent monitor. Go to Positions. The agent now watches your Greeks continuously. You don't need to stare at the screen — the agent interrupts you if and only if action is needed.
3
Respond to adjustment alerts. When a red alert appears at the top of Positions, click "Review 3 adjustments". Each option shows cost, new max loss, new delta, and win probability after adjustment. Choose one and approve. The executor handles the rest.
4
Exit before your cutoff. Your ruleset has a 3:00 PM exit rule. The agent reminds you at 2:50 PM if positions are still open. Use "Exit all" or exit individual legs from the positions table.
Post-marketAfter 3:30 PM — what to do

2
Review weekly patterns. After 5+ sessions the agent surfaces behavioural patterns like "you exit too early on Thursdays" or "your adjustment response time improved 60% this week." These help you improve systematically without manual journaling.
How to read a recommendation
The 5 parts of every recommendationImportant
1
Reasoning chain — why this strategy, why these specific strikes, why now. If the reasoning doesn't make sense to you, don't approve. The reasoning is the most important part — it's how you verify the agent's logic.
2
Backtest panel — historical performance of this exact setup under similar IV conditions. Win rate, avg profit/loss, max drawdown, profit factor. Based on real historical option chain data.
3
Metrics bar — live calculations: Max profit, Max loss, Margin required, Theta per day, Win probability. These update in real time from current option prices.
4
Legs strip — exact contracts, quantities, expected premiums. Click "Modify strikes / qty" to change any parameter. The agent recalculates all metrics instantly after modification.
5
Approve & place button — the gate. Nothing executes until you tap this. The agent never places orders without your explicit tap. Not on modification, not on watchlist alert, not ever.
Kill switch
The kill switch in the top bar is your emergency brake. Activating it immediately halts all agent activity — no new orders will be placed and all pending agent actions are cancelled. Your existing positions remain open (the kill switch does not exit positions — use "Exit all" in Positions for that). Use it if you suspect a system error, if market conditions change dramatically, or if you simply want to pause the agent. Always one click away regardless of which screen you're on.
Frequently asked questions
Does the agent ever trade without my approval?
No, never. The agent analyses, recommends, and prepares — but execution always requires your explicit tap on the Approve button. The only exception is partial-fill resolution, where the executor may adjust limit prices within ±₹2 on a pending order to complete a fill it has already initiated.
How does the agent know my risk parameters?
You set them in Strategy Setup. The agent stores your max risk per trade, daily loss limit, preferred instruments, strategy style, and sit-out conditions. Every recommendation is filtered through these rules. Update them anytime — the agent recalibrates immediately.
What happens if a leg doesn't fill?
The executor monitors fills for 90 seconds. If a leg is unfilled, the agent first adjusts the limit price within ±₹2. If still unfilled after 90s, it alerts you and presents two options: retry at market price, or unwind filled legs to avoid an orphaned position. It will never leave a half-executed strategy without alerting you immediately.
What does the backtest data come from?
The backtest finds instances in the last 2 years where market conditions (IV percentile, PCR, OI concentration) were similar to today. Win rate and P&L figures reflect actual historical option premium behaviour. The number of occurrences is always shown so you can judge the sample size.
